GPA is the cumulative score of a person's grades throughout their high school years. GPA is calculated by class weight. Most classes are on a scale of 4 with an

  • A being 4
  • B being a 3
  • C being a 2
  • D being a 1
  • F being a 0
However, some classes are rated on a scale of 4.5 or 5. For every scale 1 point is deducted for every letter grade below an A. For example, if a person got a B on a 5 point scale, they would get a 4.

Students can take even more challenging classes called AP classes which are graded on a 5 point scale. If that is not available it is reccomended to take simpler classes and achieve higher grades

Gpa is used to see where a person stands in their class. Many colleges look at gpa to determine if the student performed good enough to get into their school. Coaches also use gpa to determine if their student athlete is doing good enough in their classes to participate.
